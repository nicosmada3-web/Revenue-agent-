Phase 1 · Weeks 1–4

Clear the runway

Goal: get the foundations right, bank early wins, and build momentum — with zero sense of being on a "diet."
Week 1
Baseline & the honest picture

GP health check and your starting numbers (see below). We do an honest audit of food and drink — no changes yet, just an accurate picture of a normal week. The only thing we add: protein at breakfast, and a short daily walk on top of farm work.

Week 2
Protein locked, drink rule in

Protein at every meal now — it fills you up and protects the muscle you've got, so we're never running on empty. We bring in one simple drinking rule (no cold turkey — just structure). This is usually where the scale first moves and it starts to feel real.

Your first hero meals
Week 3

We start building your "hero meals" early — 2–3 go-to dinners you actually enjoy, that fill you up, are easy to cook after a long day, and need zero thinking. We also tackle the post-work grazing — the bit that quietly undoes a good day on the farm.

Week 4
Lock it in & review

Round the hero meals out to 5–6 you can rotate so you never stare at the fridge. We review your numbers against week 1, see what's working, smooth out what isn't, and set the runway for Phase 2.

The win: the scale moves inside the first fortnight, you've got real meals you like on repeat, and it already feels less like a diet and more like a routine.
02
Phase 2 · Weeks 5–8

Tighten the inputs

Goal: turn the early wins into steady, real fat loss.
  • Nutrition: refine portions and timing around the hero meals you've already settled into — small dials, not big sacrifices.
  • Drink: put a number and a cap on it, and swap the highest-calorie stuff (cider) for lower-cal options on the days you do drink.
  • Movement: add a bit of easy conditioning you enjoy — to keep you burning, not to break you.
The win: clothes fitting better, moving better on the pitch, recovering quicker after games.
03
Phase 3 · Weeks 9–12

Make it stick

Goal: lock it in so it survives without me watching.
  • Nutrition & drink: by now it's habit, not effort. We stress-test it — a bad week, a piss-up, a harvest crunch — and prove you can get back on without spiralling.
  • Movement: map the rugby off-season as the big fat-loss window — when you can push hardest without juggling games.
The win: you're down a meaningful chunk, you know exactly how you did it, and for the first time you can see it staying gone.
Why it's light on the gym

This makes rugby better

You're already active — 80-minute rugby plus physical farm work. Piling a heavy gym split on top adds injury risk and load to a heart that's already working hard, for very little extra fat loss. The lever is the kitchen, not the gym.

Movement's job here is to keep you moving and burning — not to break you. We keep rugby as the thing you love, and the whole point of this is to make it better: lighter, faster, lasting the full 80 with something left in the tank.

● Non-negotiable
