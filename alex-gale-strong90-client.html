The 90 days

How it runs

Phase 1 · Weeks 1–4

Clear the runway

Easy early wins, zero sense of deprivation, build momentum.
  • Health check first — the green light before anything changes (more below).
  • Nutrition: protein at every meal — fills you up, protects the muscle you've got. Sort breakfast and lunch first. No banned foods, no calorie counting.
  • Drink: no cold turkey. An honest audit, then one simple rule.
  • Movement: nothing new and heavy — just daily walking on top of farm work and rugby.
The win: the scale moves in week 1–2. You feel it. You're in.
Phase 2 · Weeks 5–8

Tighten the inputs

Turn the easy wins into real fat loss.
  • Nutrition: build your "hero meals" — 5–6 go-to dinners you actually like, repeatable, no thinking required. Tackle the evening meal and the post-work grazing.
  • Drink: a number and a cap. Swap the highest-calorie stuff for lower-cal options on the days you do drink.
  • Movement: a bit of easy conditioning you enjoy — to keep you burning, not to break you.
The win: clothes fitting better, moving better on the pitch, recovering quicker after games.
Phase 3 · Weeks 9–12

Make it stick

Lock it in so it survives without me watching.
  • Nutrition & drink: by now it's habit, not effort. We stress-test it — a bad week, a piss-up, a harvest crunch — and prove you can get back on without spiralling.
  • Movement: map the rugby off-season as the big fat-loss window — when you can push hardest without juggling games.
The win: you're down a meaningful chunk, you know exactly how you did it, and for the first time you can see it staying gone.
Why it's light on the gym

This makes rugby better

You're already active — 80-minute rugby plus physical farm work. Piling a heavy gym split on top adds injury risk and load to a heart that's already working hard, for very little extra fat loss. The lever is the kitchen, not the gym.

Movement's job here is to keep you moving and burning — not to break you. We keep rugby as the thing you love, and the whole point of this is to make it better: lighter, faster, lasting the full 80 with something left in the tank.

Non-negotiable

Health check, first

Before we change anything about your activity, you get a GP check and the green light. At your weight, with rugby on top, this isn't admin — it's the responsible move, and it protects us both.

Your baseline numbers — blood pressure, cholesterol, blood sugar — also give us a "before" to beat. This is part of doing it properly. It's not a hurdle. It's the starting line.
