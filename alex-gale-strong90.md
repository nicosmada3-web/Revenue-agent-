## THE HOOK
*How you open the plan conversation — say it close to word for word.*

> "Alex — you just played a full season of 80-minute rugby at the wrong side of 24 stone. Do you know how strong you have to be to do that? Most blokes couldn't walk a mile at your weight, and you were scrummaging for 80 minutes most weeks. That's the bit everyone misses — including you.
>
> **You don't have a fitness problem. You've never had a fitness problem.** And that's exactly why nothing's ever stuck. Every diet, every CrossFit phase — you were trying to fix a problem you don't have. The weight isn't coming from a lack of graft. It's coming from your knife, your fork, and your glass.
>
> And those three things are the easiest in the world to fix once someone shows you how. That's why, this time, it actually stays off."

**Why it lands:** it shows you know him, it tells him his past failures weren't his fault, and it points at a cause he can actually do something about. That's the difference between "here we go again" and "right, let's go."

**Back it up with the cider maths** (this is the second "he knows his stuff" moment):
> "Few pints of cider on a weekend is the same as eating a full extra day of food, every week. You'd never sit down and eat that — but you'll drink it without noticing. That's not willpower. That's just maths nobody's shown you."

**The longevity close** (the bit that makes a 32-year-old act now):
> "You're 32. This is the easiest it'll ever be to sort. Deal with it now while it's a choice — or wait, and one day it's a doctor telling you, not me asking you."

## WHAT WE'RE ACTUALLY FIXING

He's strong as a bull. We don't need to build him — we need to lighten him. So this is **not a lifting programme.** The whole job is two levers:

1. **Food** — too much, and too much of the wrong stuff.
2. **Beer / cider.**

Get those two right and the weight comes off and *stays* off, because we never starved him to get there.

---

## THE TARGET & THE HONEST TIMELINE

- **From:** 150–160kg (~24–25 stone) → **To:** 125–130kg (~20 stone).
- **That's the destination, not the 90 days.** Be straight with him: losing it properly is a 12–18 month job, not a quick fix.
- **What 90 days actually does:** proves the system works, gets the first stone or two off (the early weeks move fastest at his size), and builds habits that survive a bad week, a harvest, and a rugby season. Ninety days is where he goes from "trying" to "this is just how I live now."

Selling him a number in 90 days is what everyone else does. Selling him *a way of living that lasts* is what makes you different.

## THE 90 DAYS

### Phase 1 — Weeks 1–4: Clear the runway
*Goal: easy early wins, zero sense of deprivation, build trust.*
- **Health check first (non-negotiable — see below).**
- **Nutrition:** protein at every meal — fills him up, protects the muscle he's got. Sort breakfast and lunch first (the easy wins). No banned foods yet, no calorie counting. Just: protein first, then the rest.
- **Drink:** no cold-turkey. Honest audit — how much, when. Then one simple rule (e.g. drinking days only after games / weekends).
- **Movement:** nothing new and heavy. Add daily walking on top of farm work and rugby — easy on the heart, burns fat, near-zero injury risk.
- **The win:** scale moves in week 1–2 (water + early loss). He feels it. He's in.

### Phase 2 — Weeks 5–8: Tighten the inputs
*Goal: turn the easy wins into real fat loss.*
- **Nutrition:** build his "hero meals" — 5–6 go-to dinners he actually likes, repeatable, no thinking required. Tackle the evening meal and the post-work grazing — that's where the day is won or lost for a farmer.
- **Drink:** put a number on it and a cap. Swap the highest-calorie stuff (cider) for lower-cal options on the days he does drink.
- **Movement:** add some easy conditioning he enjoys — keeps him engaged and burning, *not* a programme that loads a heart already working hard. Light strength only if he wants it, to stay in the habit.
- **The win:** clothes fitting better, moving better on the pitch, recovering quicker after games.

### Phase 3 — Weeks 9–12: Make it stick
*Goal: lock it in so it survives without you watching.*
- **Nutrition & drink:** by now it's habit, not effort. Stress-test it — a bad week, a piss-up, a harvest crunch — and prove he can get back on without spiralling. That resilience IS "keeping it off."
- **Movement:** map the **rugby off-season as the big fat-loss window** — that's when he can push hardest without juggling games. Set the glide path toward 125–130.
- **The win:** he's down a meaningful chunk, he knows exactly how he did it, and for the first time he can see it staying gone.

## TRAINING PHILOSOPHY (why it's light-touch)

He's already active — 80-min rugby plus physical farm work. Piling a heavy gym split on top adds injury risk and load to a strained heart for very little extra fat loss. The lever is the kitchen, not the gym. Movement's job here is to **keep him engaged and burning**, not to break him. Keep rugby as the thing he loves and frame all of this as *making rugby better* — lighter, faster, lasting the full 80 with something in the tank.

---

## THE NON-NEGOTIABLE: HEALTH CHECK FIRST

Before any change in activity, Alex gets a GP check and the green light — same hard line you hold with Barry. At his weight, with the heart under strain and rugby on top, this isn't admin, it's the responsible move, and it protects you both. Baseline numbers (blood pressure, cholesterol, blood sugar) also give him a wake-up and give you a "before" to beat. Frame it to him as part of doing it *properly* — not a hurdle, a starting line.
